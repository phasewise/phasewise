Phasewise is a vertical SaaS for landscape architecture firms. It handles the operations side of the practice — phases, budgets, timesheets, submittals, MWELO compliance, and billing — so the design work isn't constantly interrupted by admin.

## What Phasewise replaces

If you currently run a stack like Monograph + Harvest + QuickBooks + spreadsheets, Phasewise replaces the first two. It sits alongside QuickBooks for accounting (we don't replace that). One subscription instead of three logins.

What we **don't** replace: AutoCAD, Land F/X, SketchUp, Bluebeam, Lumion, Adobe Acrobat, Microsoft 365. Phasewise is the operations layer, not a design tool.

## First 10 minutes after signup

After you sign up and complete checkout, you'll land on the dashboard. There are four things worth doing in your first session:

### 1. Add your team

Go to **Settings → Team management**. Click **Add member** for each staff member. Setting their LA-specific job title (Senior Associate, Landscape Architect, Designer, etc.) auto-populates a sensible billing rate and salary default — you can override either. Each invite generates a shareable link the staff member uses to set their password.

### 2. Configure your firm's billing info

Go to **Settings → Billing info**. Enter your remit-to mailing address, EIN/Fed ID, and ACH or wire details if you want them printed on invoices. This information renders on every invoice you send so clients know how to pay.

### 3. Create your first project

Go to **Projects → New Project**. The form prompts you for project name, client, contract fee, start date, and the phases you want to track (PreD, SD, DD, CD, BID, CA, POC are pre-loaded). Each phase gets its own budget so you can see burn rates per phase, not just per project.

### 4. Log your first time entry

Go to **Time Sheets**. Pick the project + phase, enter hours per day across the week. Click Submit at the end of the week to send to your supervisor for approval. Approved hours flow into invoices automatically.

## What to do next

Once you have a project and a few approved time entries, the real value shows up:

- **Dashboard** shows budget burn per project at a glance — green/amber/red flags as projects approach 90% of budget.
- **Reports** show profitability per project, per phase, and per person.
- **Admin → Project Billing** lets you generate invoices that auto-pull approved hours.
